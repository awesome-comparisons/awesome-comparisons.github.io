---
title: Use Cases
description: What each operating system is typically used for.
---

## Linux

Dominates servers, cloud infrastructure, and embedded devices, and is a popular choice for developers on the desktop, thanks to its openness, configurability, and closeness to production server environments.

## Windows

The dominant desktop OS for general home and enterprise use, PC gaming, and business software that depends on decades of Windows-only legacy applications.

## macOS

Popular among creative professionals (design, video, audio) and developers who want a Unix-based system with polished, tightly integrated hardware and software.

## iOS

The default choice for a large share of premium smartphones and tablets, valued for its consistent performance, long software-update support, and tightly curated app ecosystem.

## Android

The most widely used mobile OS globally, spanning everything from budget phones to flagships, thanks to its openness, hardware diversity, and adoption by manufacturers worldwide.

## ChromeOS

Common in education and budget laptops, and increasingly in business settings, wherever most work happens in a browser and low maintenance, fast boot times, and built-in security matter more than native app flexibility.
