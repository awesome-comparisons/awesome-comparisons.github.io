title: History
description: The origin of each operating system — who created it, when, and why.
---

## Linux

Started by Linus Torvalds in 1991 as a free kernel for his own PC, released to the internet and combined with the GNU project's existing userland tools to form a complete, freely redistributable Unix-like operating system.

## Windows

Launched by Microsoft in 1985 as a graphical shell over MS-DOS, later rebuilt on the entirely new Windows NT kernel (1993) that eventually became the foundation for every modern release, including Windows 10 and 11.

## macOS

Introduced by Apple in 2001 as Mac OS X, a from-scratch successor to the classic Mac OS built on NeXTSTEP (acquired with Steve Jobs' return to Apple in 1997) and the Unix-based Darwin/XNU kernel.

## iOS

Introduced by Apple in 2007 alongside the original iPhone, derived from the same Darwin/XNU foundation as macOS but rebuilt with a touch-first interface and a far more locked-down application model.

## Android

Developed by Android Inc. (founded 2003) and acquired by Google in 2005, launched commercially in 2008 as an open-source, Linux-kernel-based mobile OS designed to compete with the newly announced iPhone.

## ChromeOS

Announced by Google in 2009 and launched in 2011, built on the Linux kernel and originally conceived as a lightweight OS where the Chrome browser was effectively the entire user interface.
