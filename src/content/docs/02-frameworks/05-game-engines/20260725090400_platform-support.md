---
title: Platform Support
description: What platforms each game engine can target.
---

## Unity

One of the broadest platform reaches of any engine: desktop, mobile (iOS/Android), all major consoles, WebGL, and VR/AR, a major reason for its popularity in mobile gaming specifically.

## Unreal Engine

Broad platform support across desktop, consoles, mobile, and VR, though its heavier default fidelity and resource footprint make it comparatively less common for lightweight mobile titles.

## Godot

Supports desktop, mobile, web (via WebAssembly), and consoles (through third-party porting partners rather than official first-party support), a rapidly improving but historically smaller platform footprint than Unity or Unreal.

## GameMaker

Supports desktop, mobile, web, and consoles, with console export requiring the separate Enterprise subscription tier rather than being available on the base commercial license.

## Construct

Primarily targets web and mobile through HTML5 export, with console support more limited than the general-purpose 3D engines in this group.

## CryEngine

Supports desktop and consoles, with historically less emphasis on mobile compared to Unity or Cocos Creator.

## Cocos Creator

Strong mobile and web platform support, with growing desktop and mini-game platform support (particularly for platforms like WeChat mini-games popular in China).

## Bevy

Supports desktop and web (via WebAssembly) today, with console support still an early-stage, community-driven effort rather than an official, polished offering.

## Defold

Supports desktop, mobile, and web, deliberately keeping a small, consistent footprint across all of them rather than chasing console support.
