---
title: Licensing and Pricing
description: How each game engine is licensed and priced.
---

## Unity

Free (Personal tier) up to $200,000 in trailing revenue/funding, then paid per-seat Pro/Enterprise subscriptions; the widely criticized 2023 per-install "Runtime Fee" was cancelled in September 2024 and hasn't returned.

## Unreal Engine

Free to download and use, with a 5% royalty on lifetime gross revenue only after a game crosses $1 million — meaning most indie developers never pay anything; non-game commercial use has a separate seat-based license.

## Godot

Completely free and open source under the permissive MIT license, with no royalties, revenue thresholds, or paid tiers of any kind, governed by the nonprofit Godot Foundation.

## GameMaker

Free for non-commercial use on non-console platforms, with a one-time roughly $99.99 fee for a commercial license on non-console platforms; console export requires a separate ongoing Enterprise subscription.

## Construct

A subscription-based license (with a limited free tier), priced per year rather than tied to game revenue or royalties.

## CryEngine

Available under a "pay what you want" model with a small minimum, plus a 5% royalty on revenue after a project earns its first $5,000 — one of the more unusual pricing structures in this group.

## Cocos Creator

Free and open source for its core engine, with optional paid cloud/backend services offered separately by its maintainer, Cocos.

## Bevy

Completely free and open source, dual-licensed under MIT/Apache 2.0, with no royalties, funded through donations and grants via the nonprofit Bevy Foundation.

## Defold

Completely free with no royalties or revenue thresholds of any kind, stewarded by the nonprofit Defold Foundation rather than sold as a commercial product.
