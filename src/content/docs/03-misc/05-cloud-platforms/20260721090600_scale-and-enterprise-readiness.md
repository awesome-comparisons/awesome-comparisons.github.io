---
title: Scale and Enterprise Readiness
description: How well-suited each cloud/hosting platform is to large-scale or regulated enterprise workloads.
---

## AWS

The default choice for large enterprises, with the deepest bench of compliance certifications, enterprise support plans, and dedicated account teams of any provider on this list.

## GCP

Enterprise-ready with strong compliance coverage, particularly favored by data-heavy organizations already invested in Google's analytics and ML tooling.

## Azure

Often the preferred hyperscaler for large enterprises already standardized on Microsoft, with deep compliance coverage and government/sovereign-cloud options.

## Vercel

Offers an Enterprise plan with SSO, dedicated support, and SLAs, but is generally chosen for frontend/web workloads rather than as a full enterprise infrastructure platform.

## Cloudflare

Enterprise-ready at the network and security layer in particular (DDoS protection, WAF, Zero Trust), increasingly extending that enterprise story to Workers and its data products.

## Netlify

Offers an Enterprise tier with SSO and dedicated support, similar in scope to Vercel's enterprise offering and aimed at the same frontend-focused audience.

## DigitalOcean

Generally positioned for startups, small teams, and individual developers rather than large regulated enterprises, though it does offer some enterprise support options.

## Render

Offers an Enterprise plan with SSO and compliance features, but remains best suited to small-to-mid-sized teams rather than large-scale regulated workloads.

## Fly.io

Primarily aimed at startups and small-to-mid teams; enterprise features exist but the platform is not typically the choice for large, heavily regulated organizations.

## Heroku

Following Salesforce's 2026 shift to a "sustainability model," new customers no longer get access to Enterprise Account contracts, and the platform's enterprise roadmap has been effectively frozen.
